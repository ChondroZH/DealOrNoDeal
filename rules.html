üéÆ Game Overview

Deal or No Deal is a game of luck and strategy. You'll face 10 briefcases, each containing a different cash prize. Your goal is to either win the highest prize possible or make a deal with the Banker at the right time!

üí∞ Prize Values

The 10 cases contain the following prize values:

$1 $5 $10 $20 $30
$50 $80 $100 $200 $300

üìù How to Play

Step 1: Choose Your Case

At the start of the game, select one case from the 10 available. This is YOUR case - you'll keep it until the end of the game. You won't know what's inside until the very end!

Step 2: Open Cases in Rounds

The game is played in 3 rounds. In each round, you'll open a specific number of cases:

  • Round 1: Open 3 cases
  • Round 2: Open 3 cases
  • Round 3: Open 2 cases

As you open cases, their prize values are revealed and eliminated from the board. You're hoping to eliminate the low values while keeping the high values in play!

Step 3: The Banker's Offer

After each round, the mysterious Banker will make you an offer to buy your case. The offer is based on the remaining prizes still in play.

You have two choices:

  • DEAL: Accept the Banker's offer and end the game with that amount
  • NO DEAL: Reject the offer and continue playing for a chance at a higher prize

Step 4: The Final Decision

After Round 3, if you reject the Banker's offer, you'll be left with just two cases: your original case and one other. At this point, you must make the final choice:

  • KEEP: Stick with your original case
  • SWITCH: Trade your case for the other remaining case

Once you decide, both cases will be revealed and you'll see what you've won!

üéØ Strategy Tips

  • The Banker's offer is usually influenced by the average of the remaining prizes
  • If you eliminate high-value cases early, expect lower offers
  • If low-value cases are eliminated, the Banker's offers will increase
  • There's no "right" answer - it's all about risk vs. reward!
  • Trust your instincts and have fun!

üèÜ Winning

You win by either accepting a Banker's offer or revealing your case at the end. The highest possible win is $300, but any prize is a win!

Remember: This is a game of luck and nerves. Do you have what it takes to beat the Banker?
